LISA'S BACKGROUND

Some may call my work and personal history intriguing while others may refer to it as bizarre. I belive it is somewhere in the middle. Here's some fun history for you to better understand where I come from, who I am, and how my unique background got me where I am today.

  • Childhood
    Grew up moving up and down the west coast of the United States, finally settled in Portland, Oregon.
  • The Teenage years
    In high school I was certain I would be a doctor or a teacher. My favorite subject was AP Biology. I was Captain of the Dance Team (in my case this sport was for the nerds of the school). I developed a back injury in my Senior Year and learned I have an extra vertebrae. How's that for a random fact! Throughout high school I enjoyed many jobs such as babysitting, catering, ice cream scooper, and more!
  • University...6 Beautiful Years
    I attended Oregon State University beginning with a major in Nutrition and a minor in Spanish - intention was to move on to medical school (there was no pre-med major available). I changed courses after a couple of years, switching my major to Early Childhood Education. Afer studying abroad in Spain and finishing the requirements for my minor I realized I didn't feel confident in my Spanish knowledge and decided to pursue a second degree in Spanish to further hone my skills. One requirement for this Spanish degree was to take a couple years of an additional language - in my case, italiano. I was fortunate enough to have a second study abroad experience in Italy where I fell in love with the people, culture, and let's not kid ourselves, the food of this beautiful part of the world. Ultimately I graduated with a B.A. in Spanish and a B.S. in Humand Development & Family Sciences (focus on Early Childhood Education). My Friday nights in college were spent babysitting and completing homework - I was so much fun!
  • A Passion is Born (who knew it would shape my future)
    During college I was also able to take PADI SCUBA certification as an elective. I was invited to spend 10 days in Belize the following summer and BOOM another passion was born.
  • Young Adulthood
    After graduating I realized that spending all of my working hours with children would quickly burn me out and make it more difficult to enjoy my future children. After a brief stint as a nanny on the east coast I decided to switch gears and switch to Conference Management / hotel & hospitality work.
  • Ciao!
    During a business trip to Reno I met a woman from Italy who is now a dear friend. I explained to her that my life's dream was to become Italian. She invited me to come live with her and her boyfriend in Bologna, Italy while I searched for work. I bought a one way ticket and lived in their pantry while traveling the country and interviewing at hotels. Ultimately I decided the US was my home and I returned in time to help my nephew get ready for halloween as Captain Jack Sparrow. Aaaaaahhhh those were good times.
  • Back to My Roots
    Upon my return to the US I decided to return to my roots and begin working for Early Head Start. I will leave out the gory details but after some incredible years with this work I switched gears when I found an opening for an 80% travel position for a wind turbine manufacturer.
  • The Corporate World
    After 11.5 years with this company and seeing parts of the world I never would have seen without this opportunity, it was time (way past time?) to part ways with the corporate world. I had realized that I had knack for ERP systems and quickly became a superuser. During this time I also honed my skills in customer service, learned a touch about engineering, and most importantly had children.
  • The Best of Belize
    The next big adventure was starting my own business and working as a contractor doing reservations for a resort in Belize. Who knew that this would be a perfect culmination of my skills. But alas, the reactive nature of the work was wearing on me and my work-life balance so I thought...I have loved dabbling in html, why not give it a try!

And alas... I found Treehouse Tech Degree program. After years of a dear friend encouraging me to pursue the tech industry I finally started on this path. I couldn't be happier or more excited about the skills I am learning. My hope is to use my abundance (if not random) of experience with technical skills, customer service, communication, marketing, and more to be a contractor in the tech world. If I'm being perfectly honest I have an other passion - data. So hopefully data science is also in my future, once I master Front End Web design. There you have it - more information than you would have asked for or expected. Looking forward to the opportunity to put my new and old skills to use!
